Open issues · flight blockers

Two fixes to fly

MOSFETS · RLZ44N are standard-level, won't fully saturate on a 3.3V gate. Swap to IRLZ44N / AO3400 (logic-level).
POWER · 650mAh 1C pack current-starves the motors (~10A peak demand). Move to a high-C 1S LiPo, e.g. Tattu 500mAh 95C. Verify JST-PHR-2P first.

HORIZON

ableLion V1

The first real solo entry in the line. Go brushless, stay in full software control, keep the ESP32 and your own flight code. Budget ≈ $105 to $125.

Airframe + power

2S brushless, ~85mm

  • 4× 1103 11000KV motors
  • 40mm 3-blade props
  • 2S 300 to 450mAh LiPo packs
  • 2S balance charger
The critical constraint

Standalone ESC, not an AIO

  • Micro 4-in-1 ESC, 2S, 10 to 20A
  • Must expose S1 to S4 signal pads
  • AM32 / BLHeli_S firmware
  • 1S brushless means AIO lock-in. 2S is the floor.
Software work

ESP32 to ESC

  • DShot out via RMT peripheral
  • ESC arming sequence
  • Full PID retune for brushless

Operating principles, earned not assumed

C-rating beats capacity on small high-draw builds. A smaller high-C pack outflies a bigger low-C one when motors are current-starved.

MOSFET logic level is non-negotiable. A 3.3V GPIO won't open a standard-level part, so use logic-level silicon for ESP32-driven motors.

1S brushless means AIO lock-in. Anything small enough at 1S hides the ESC signal pads. 2S buys back software control.

Verify the connector before you buy. Custom soldered builds don't always match "standard" parts.
