Mini Drone

An 85mm 3D-printed brushed quadcopter running flight code written from scratch on an ESP32. Built as a CICS 256 group project, so it is not a solo claim, but it is the bench that taught every lesson the rest of the line stands on.

Open issues · what stands between it and first flight

1 · MOSFET gate drive

The RLZ44N are standard-level MOSFETs. A 3.3V GPIO from the ESP32 can't fully saturate the gate, which wants closer to 10V, so the motors never get full drive. Fix: swap to logic-level parts, IRLZ44N or AO3400.

2 · Current starvation

The original ~650mAh 1C pack can only deliver about 0.65A, while four 8520 motors demand up to 10A peak. Result: no lift. Fix: high-discharge 1S LiPo, ~350 to 550mAh at 30C or higher. Tattu 500mAh 95C is a strong match. Verify the JST-PHR-2P connector against the existing wiring first, and use a standard 3.7V LiPo (not LiHV) to avoid charger issues.

What it taught

C-rating beats capacity on small high-draw builds. A smaller high-C pack outflies a bigger low-C one when the motors are current-starved.

MOSFET logic level is non-negotiable. A 3.3V GPIO won't open a standard-level part. ESP32-driven motors need logic-level silicon.

Verify the connector before you buy. A custom soldered build doesn't always match a "standard" part.
